# Shared context: US premium ice cream market (2026)

Use this evidence to ground "Wedge & market evidence" and "Moat & trust posture" scoring.

## Market landscape

- US ice cream market: ~$12B retail, growing 3% annually
- Premium ($6+/pint) segment: ~$2.4B, growing 8% annually (faster than mass)
- Specialty / artisan segment: ~$400M, growing 14% annually (fastest growing)

## Direct competitors

| Competitor | Positioning | Approx revenue | Why they would NOT ship the contesting idea | Why they MIGHT |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Häagen-Dazs (General Mills)** | Mass premium | $1.2B globally | Scale prevents single-origin sourcing; "vanilla" already captured | Could acquire a single-origin brand |
| **Ben & Jerry's (Unilever)** | Mass premium + values | $700M | Optimizes for fun mix-ins, not minimalism | Could collab with single-origin chocolate brand |
| **Breyers (Unilever)** | Mass classic | $500M | Distribution moat; not premium | Could sell single-origin via Unilever's premium label |
| **Salt & Straw** | Scoop shops + DTC | ~$80M | Scoop-shop-first business model; small grocery presence | Could expand grocery line |
| **Jeni's Splendid** | Mid-premium grocery | ~$60M | Broad-flavor strategy, not single-origin | Could add origin-focused line |
| **Van Leeuwen** | NYC lifestyle premium | ~$50M | Lifestyle brand, not origin-specialty | Likely to copy if successful |
| **McConnell's** | West Coast craft | ~$25M | Small-batch focus; vanilla-bean is signature | Likely to defend vanilla territory |

## Adjacent / cautionary comps

- **Three Twins Ice Cream** (organic premium, dead 2018): scaled too fast, distribution costs killed margins
- **Steve's Ice Cream** (artisan, dead 2017): premium positioning didn't match grocery economics
- **Coolhaus** (architectural ice cream): pivoted from artisan to mass-market, lost differentiation
- **Bi-Rite** (SF artisan, sustainable): never scaled past single store, but profitable

## Demand evidence

- Whole Foods reports 18% YoY growth in $7+/pint ice cream sales (2025)
- Single-origin chocolate bar sales: $850M/yr, growing 12% (Tcho, Dandelion, Theo all profitable)
- Reddit r/icecream + Bon Appétit cover specialty ice cream every quarter
- DTC ice cream subscription startups: 4 funded since 2024 (avg $5M seed); 2 already shut down
- "Vanilla bean" Google search trend stable, no growth signal
- "Single-origin chocolate" search trend +40% YoY

## Pricing anchors

- $3-5/qt: mass-market (Breyers, store brand)
- $7-9/pint: premium (Häagen-Dazs, Ben & Jerry's signature)
- $9-11/pint: artisan (Jeni's, Van Leeuwen)
- $30+/mo: DTC subscription (proven willingness)

## Demand anti-evidence

- Vanilla as a standalone brand (rather than as part of a multi-flavor lineup) has no proven precedent at premium pricing
- Single-origin ice cream specifically has no $50M+ brand yet
- Specialty ice cream subscription churn is ~60% by month 3 in early DTC data
