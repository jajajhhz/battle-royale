# Chocolate Ice Cream — A Premium Single-Origin Brand

## One-line value proposition

A single-origin dark chocolate ice cream made from 70% Ecuadorian cacao — the chocolate equivalent of single-origin coffee.

## Target user

- Specialty grocers (Whole Foods, Erewhon, Bristol Farms) and their shoppers
- Foodies who pay $14 for a single-origin chocolate bar
- Restaurants offering "chef's selection" desserts
- Gifting market — premium ice cream as a small luxury

## Felt user value

You're in the freezer aisle but you don't want childhood ice cream. You want the dessert version of your $7 cortado: a moment of intentional indulgence, made from ingredients with stories you can read on the back.

## v1 scope

8oz pints (not 1.5qt — premium positioning). 70% Ecuadorian cacao from a single estate. 16% butterfat. Hand-poured, small-batch. Available at 50 specialty grocers in CA, NY, MA in 6 months.

## Pricing / business model

$8.99 retail per 8oz pint (= ~$36/qt equivalent). Margins ~38%. Direct-store-delivery to specialty grocers + DTC subscription ($30/mo for 4 pints).

## Competitive position

- Salt & Straw owns scoop-shop premium ($8/scoop, no grocery presence)
- Jeni's owns mid-premium grocery ($10.99/pint, broader flavors)
- Häagen-Dazs Chocolate owns premium grocery ($7.99/pint, mass distribution)
- Van Leeuwen owns NYC-cool ($9.99/pint, lifestyle brand)
- We claim "single-origin" as a sliver no incumbent owns at scale.

## Defensibility / moat

Direct-trade cacao sourcing relationship (3-year exclusive with the Ecuadorian estate). Story is the brand. Once "single-origin chocolate ice cream" is associated with us, every imitator looks like a copycat. DTC subscription locks in 5,000 customers in year 1.

## Strategic future

Year 2: 3 more single-origin variants (Madagascar, Tanzania, Peru). Year 3: paired tasting boxes ("3 origins, side by side"). Year 5: build the "specialty ice cream" category like Blue Bottle built specialty coffee — eventual acquisition by a strategic (Unilever, Nestlé) at premium multiple.

## Founder fit & ship-ability

Solo founder is a former pastry chef with cacao sourcing relationships. Manufacturing partner identified (small-batch dairy in NorCal). Ship in 6 months. Cost: $400K (premium packaging + smaller initial runs).

## Risks

- "Single-origin" claim hard to verify — consumer trust takes time
- Specialty grocery margins are tight; co-op fees can be brutal
- Premium category sensitive to recession
- Imitation by Salt & Straw or Jeni's once proven
