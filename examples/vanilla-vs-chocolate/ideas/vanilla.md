# Vanilla Ice Cream — A Reliable Classic Brand

## One-line value proposition

The most-purchased flavor on Earth: a clean, classic, universally-loved ice cream that works in every dessert pairing.

## Target user

- Families buying ice cream for mixed-age groups
- Restaurants and dessert shops needing a versatile base flavor
- Consumers who want a dessert ingredient, not a standalone star
- Health-conscious buyers (subjectively perceived as "lighter")

## Felt user value

You walk into a freezer aisle. You don't want decision fatigue. You don't want to guess if the kids will like it. You don't want a flavor that fights with the apple pie. Vanilla solves all four. It is the ice cream you reach for when ice cream is the side, not the show.

## v1 scope

Standard 1.5 quart container. Madagascar bourbon vanilla. 14% butterfat. Available in every grocery store in 30 days.

## Pricing / business model

$4.99 retail. Sold through grocery distributor partnerships. Margins ~22%.

## Competitive position

- Häagen-Dazs Vanilla owns premium ($7.99, 7% market share)
- Breyers Natural Vanilla owns mass ($4.49, 23% market share)
- Generic store brand owns cheap ($2.99, 18% market share)
- We split the difference: better ingredients than Breyers, cheaper than Häagen-Dazs

## Defensibility / moat

Real vanilla bean sourcing relationship in Madagascar (5-year exclusive). Recipe simplicity makes it hard to differentiate, but distribution + brand trust is a moat.

## Strategic future

Year 2: vanilla-bean variants (vanilla custard, French vanilla, Tahitian vanilla). Year 3: vanilla as the platform flavor for partnerships (cookies & cream, ice cream cake bases). Year 5: become the default vanilla for B2B foodservice (restaurants, hotels).

## Founder fit & ship-ability

Solo founder with food-industry contacts. Recipe is locked. Manufacturing partner ready. Ship in 90 days. Cost: $200K for first production run.

## Risks

- Commoditization — anyone can make vanilla
- Distribution gatekeeping by major grocers
- Shelf-space loss to seasonal flavors
